Continuous Data Mean Deviation Experiment

Enter continuous data as Class Intervals, Frequency (F), separated by commas. Example: 0-10:2, 10-20:5, 20-30:3


Continuous Data Analysis

Formula

Median: (Sum of midpoints of intervals multiplied by their frequencies) / (Sum of frequencies).

MDME: (Sum of absolute deviations from the Median multiplied by frequencies) / (Sum of frequencies).

Calculation Process

1. Calculate the Median by multiplying each midpoint of intervals by its frequency, summing the results, and dividing by the sum of frequencies.

2. Compute the MDME by finding the absolute deviations from the Median, multiplying by frequencies, summing them, and dividing by the sum of frequencies.
